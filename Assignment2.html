From identity crisis to the Success Story - The DevOps revolution!

Kalyan Mahalingam

| 05 July 2019

DevOps is a paradigm shift in the way how software products are being built in the modern day Information Technology (IT) organizations. It is becoming the de-facto component in Software Development Lifecycle (SDLC) process in most of the organizations. But what is this all about. Here, I am exploring how DevOps can be compared to some of the similar revolutions of the past and how it is impacting the work culture in the organizations. But before I dig deeper, let’s understand what DevOps truly is - DevOps can simply be defined as the “combination of cultural philosophies, practices, and tools that increases an organization’s ability to deliver applications and services at high velocity”. DevOps is not restricted to any set of processes and tools, but is more rightly represented by the culture of enhanced communication and collaboration between Infrastructure and system operations guys and developers guided by principles and philosophies of DevOps such as Continuous Integration (CI), Continuous Delivery (CD), Infrastructure as Code, Automation and other similar practices. The different components of DevOps include - Source Code Management, Automated Build and Test System, Automated Deployments, Infrastructure Orchestration tools and many other tools and processes. If all this sounds alien, I would recommend you to watch this video by my friend and colleague here . He has talked about the DevOps philosophy and the technology in use in some of his amazing DevOps Course Videos. From CMMI & the Agile world to the DevOps era - Many Businesses have long formed the Power organizations or hierarchies and there are success stories based on those hierarchies. Organizations do not want to change or revamp their hierarchies for the only reason that technology is changing at a fast pace. Though not a huge supporter of the thought process, but I understand the school of thoughts behind the decisions. It is definitely a difficult transition. From the Business side, it needs a strong commitment and conviction to shake their organization, but on the other hand, technology or the process in question needs to be strong enough to make them rethink and revamp. Importance of Data Backup and Recovery in the IT industry

EdYoda

| 26 Aug 2019

A business must always introspect the areas where they lack in order to bring about a positive change in the work environment. One of the main precautions or a contingency plan that is necessary for every business and especially industries working with clouds, networks and computers should conduct a backup of its files which will serve as a boon in any point of time. Before moving forward, let us understand the concept of Data Backup and Recovery. Backup is the process of creating and storing copies of data that is confidential to the organization against the data loss or in case of emergency. Recovery is the process that involves restoring the lost data to the original location or another location. It is essential to have a proper backup copy in a separate system. It is the duty of every employee to keep the data structured and backed up in a separate storage device and not to hand it over to somebody else as it has confidential information.
